Manu Temple

Manu Temple is a famous religious site in Manali, Himachal Pradesh, dedicated to Sage Manu, who is believed to be the creator of the human race in Hindu mythology. Located in Old Manali, the temple is situated along the Beas River, offering a serene and picturesque setting. The temple is built in a pagoda-style architecture, with intricate wood carvings and a peaceful ambiance. It is a significant pilgrimage site and attracts both devotees and tourists. Visitors need to trek uphill to reach the temple, which adds to its charm.

Solang Valley

Solang Valley is a famous tourist destination near Manali, Himachal Pradesh, known for its stunning snow-capped mountains and adventure sports. Located about 14 km from Manali, it offers activities like paragliding, skiing, snowboarding, zorbing, and ATV rides. During winter (December to February), the valley is covered in snow, making it a popular spot for skiing and snow activities, while in summer (April to June), it becomes ideal for paragliding and trekking. The Solang Ropeway provides breathtaking views of the valley.

Hidimba Devi Temple

Hidimba Devi Temple, also known as Dhungri Temple, is a famous ancient temple in Manali, Himachal Pradesh, dedicated to Goddess Hidimba, the wife of Bhima from the Mahabharata. Built in 1553 by Maharaja Bahadur Singh, the temple is surrounded by tall cedar (deodar) trees, creating a peaceful and mystical atmosphere.
